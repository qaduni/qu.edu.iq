# Website Evaluation Criteria and Requirements
*Based on the Official Website Evaluation Form of the University of Warith Al-Anbiyaa (Electronic Computer Center - Website Division).*

This document outlines the metrics, requirements, and weights used to evaluate institutional academic websites. The total standard score is 1000 points, with up to 100 additional points for extra strengths.

---

## 1. Language Requirements (Weight: 50 Points)
* **Full Website Translation:** Complete translation of the website into English, or at least the main navigation sections/menus.
* **English Quality & Correctness:** Scientific and accurate linguistic translation.
* **Arabic Quality & Correctness:** Flawless Arabic writing, proper text editing, and freedom from typos or grammatical errors.

## 2. Domain & Official Emails (Weight: 20 Points)
* **Official Domain Name:** The website must use the official educational extension (`.edu.iq`). Standard commercial extensions (like `.com`, `.net`, `.org`) receive 0 points.
* **Official Corporate Emails:** Availability of official academic email accounts mapped to the domain (`uniname@edu.iq`).

## 3. Cumulative Statistics Page (Weight: 40 Points)
A dedicated standalone page displaying comprehensive institutional data, which must include:
* Number of colleges and departments.
* Annual cumulative performance statistics.
* Total number of faculty members.
* Total number of enrolled students.
* Total number of published scientific research papers.

## 4. Website Updates Frequency (Weight: 30 Points)
* **News Section Updates:** Fresh news coverage updated at a maximum interval of every 5 days.
* **Conferences, Seminars & Workshops Schedule:** Monthly updates of the institution's official scientific calendar.
* **Training Courses Schedule:** Monthly updates of continuous education and training plans.

## 5. Multimedia Content (Weight: 30 Points)
* **Promotional & Introductory Videos:** Video tours and introductory short films highlighting the university, its scientific laboratories, and academic departments.
* **Photo Galleries:** Structured image albums displaying the campus buildings, facilities, students, and lab equipment.
* **Media Publications:** Availability of electronic magazines, university newspapers, or university radio broadcasts.

## 6. Contact Information (Weight: 40 Points)
* **University-Level Contact Details:** Clear display of official email, telephone numbers, fax, or P.O. Box.
* **Departmental Contact Details:** Dedicated contact channels (email/mobile) for each specific department.
* **Contact Us Form:** An interactive form equipped with an automatic receipt acknowledgment feature (`auto-reply`).
* **Social Media Integration:** Direct links to active official social media handles to boost reach and engagement.

## 7. Institutional Profile & Identity (Weight: 40 Points)
* **About the University:** General background, establishment history, and the official organizational structure chart.
* **Vision, Mission, and Objectives:** Clearly defined strategic declarations for the university and its colleges.
* **Handbooks and Guidelines:** Published regulatory documents, guidelines, and bylaws for students, teaching staff, and administrative personnel.

## 8. Layout, Typography & Design (Weight: 50 Points)
* **Visual Consistency:** Uniform alignment, font types, text sizes, and color palettes across all web pages.
* **Navigation Efficiency:** Rapid loading times and seamless navigation utilizing user-friendly drop-down menus.
* **Hyperlink Integrity:** All internal and external hyperlinks must be fully functional (`Active links`), split into main links and sub-links.
* **Search Engine Sitemap:** A correctly configured technical XML Sitemap optimized for search engine indexing.

## 9. Related Websites & Academic Links (Weight: 40 Points)
* **Subdomains mapping:** Department, college, and research center websites must be systematically hosted as subdomains under the primary institutional domain.
* **Affiliated & Friendly Portals:** Direct links pointing toward ministries of higher education, scientific organizations, and vital institutions.
* **Program Specification Descriptions:** A clear academic program descriptive syllabus framework published for each department on the college's page.

## 10. Students & Alumni Support (Weight: 40 Points Each)
* **Undergraduate & Postgraduate Student Portals:**
  * Annual University Calendar.
  * Admission, registration, and graduation criteria/guidelines.
  * Admission planning figures and thresholds.
  * Honor rolls displaying top-achieving students.
* **Alumni Gateway:**
  * Direct portal communication channels for graduates.
  * Professional qualification, career placement, and employment tools.
  * Industry employer feedback/survey mechanisms.
  * Archives highlighting prominent alumni, historical scientific icons, and figures.
  * Global awards, honors, and medals won by graduates.

## 11. Faculty Profiles & Academic Publications (Weight: 40 Points & 30 Points)
* **Faculty Directory:**
  * Published course guides detailing specializations, syllabi, and credit hour units.
  * Faculty lists containing comprehensive individual Academic Profiles (`cv/profile`).
  * Dedicated direct communication channels (official emails) for each lecturer.
* **Academic Publications:**
  * Published research repositories categorized by the evaluation year.
  * Directory of institutional scientific journals along with full volume archives.
  * List of officially registered patents secured during the evaluation year.

## 12. Technical Architecture & Performance (Weight: 60 Points)
* **Dynamic Programming Framework:** The website must be dynamic, leveraging modern back-end environments (such as `PHP`, `ASP.NET`, etc.). Static websites receive 0 points.
* **Search Engine Optimization (SEO):** Clean, semantic architecture fully compliance with SEO industry benchmarks.
* **Cross-Browser Compatibility:** Flawless rendering across all mainstream modern web browsers (`Google Chrome`, `Microsoft Edge`, `Mozilla Firefox`, etc.).
* **Responsive Layout:** Complete layout fluidity adapted for flawless viewing on mobile devices, tablets, and smartphones.

## 13. Digital Services & Portals (Weight: 100 Points)
* **General Electronic Services:** Published database rosters containing graduate names/specialties, institutional email provisioning for students, and intelligent automated virtual assistants (`ChatBots`).
* **Digital Library & Resource Gateways:** Integration of dedicated e-library frameworks, comprehensive automated catalogs (e.g., `Koha`), and pathways leading toward Open Access resource indexes.
* **Student Portal:** Personalized login space providing student grade books, live lecture timetables, examination results, attendance statistics, and warning indicators.
* **Faculty Portal (Staff Portal):** Personal dashboard hosting uploaded lectures, bibliography records, active intellectual works, assigned council committees, and official commissions.

## 14. Global Ranking Benchmarks (Weight: 50 Points)
* **Webometrics Indexing Performance:** Score points allocated proportionally based on the university's official tier placement in the latest standard Webometrics World University Rankings global release. Higher tiers (e.g., Rank 1-3000) command full weight, dropping gradually for lower tiers down to 0 points if unranked.

## 15. Integrated Platforms, Repositories & Lifelong Learning (Weight: 160 Points)
* **Enterprise Interoperability Matrix:**
  * A central Learning Management System (`LMS`) cleanly tied into the primary domain.
  * A Student Information System (`SIS`) unified with the LMS.
  * Operational Enterprise Resource Planning (`ERP`) tools managing financial and human capital.
  * Native smartphone applications available on iOS App Store and Android Google Play.
  * Scientific Promotion dashboards tracking academic career advancements.
  * Event management portals scheduling seminars, workshops, and scientific activities.
  * Digital publishing pipelines managing peer-reviewed university journals.
* **Digital Repositories:** A robust digital repository consolidating institutional digital assets (intellectual outputs, research, lecture presentations, historical documents).
* **Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs):** Dedicated home-grown MOOC engines, combined with active API/institutional integration setups with international platforms (such as `Coursera`, `edX`) or global academies.

## 16. Security, Privacy & Cyber Defense (Weight: 40 Points)
* **Data Encryption Protocols:** Strict adoption of secure cryptographic connection routing via `HTTPS` backed by valid, active `SSL` certificates.
* **Firewall Infrastructure:** Deploying hardware/software web application firewalls to mitigate cyber threats.
* **User Data Governance:** Explicitly defined, publicly posted Privacy Policies and User Safety protocols.
* **Cyber Penetration Resilience:** Hardened codebase fully resilient against common cyber exploits and vulnerabilities (such as `DDoS` attacks, `SQL Injection`, `XSS`, etc.).

---

## Extra Strengths Points (Bonus Weight: Up to +100 Points)
These are bonus elements credited directly over the base evaluation score:
1. **International Student Gateway (20 Pts):** A dedicated, localized entrance interface addressing international applicants (`Study in Iraq` initiatives).
2. **Sustainable Development Goals Tracking (20 Pts):** Dedicated channels and web sections specifically mapping and documenting institutional contributions toward United Nations `SDGs`.
3. **Periodic Penetration Testing (20 Pts):** Subjecting the network infrastructure to recurring certified white-hat cyber attacks and responding proactively to detected vulnerabilities.
4. **University Ecosystem Incubators (20 Pts):** Practical linkages embedded in the platform connecting emerging graduates directly with business incubators and corporate industries.
5. **Creative/Proprietary Innovations (20 Pts):** Exceptional, unique cutting-edge digital additions or features implemented exclusively by the university that distinguish it from standard academic platforms.
